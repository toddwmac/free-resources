Lost & Found

A Report on Pharmaceutical Redemptions (1918–2025)

This report identifies six medicinal plants that were dismissed as "useless" or "feeble" by the Dispensatory of the United States in 1918, but have since been validated by modern science or become pillars of 21st-century healthcare.

Wild Yam

Dioscorea villosa
The 1918 Verdict

It has no medicinal properties, however, beyond a feeble diuretic action...

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 442
The 2025 Reality
Status: Essential Precursor

Far from having "no properties," this plant was found to contain diosgenin. This compound became the starting material for synthesizing progesterone and cortisone, revolutionizing medicine by enabling the mass production of the birth control pill and anti-inflammatory steroids. (Source: Steroid Synthesis Breakthrough)

Echinacea

Echinacea angustifolia
The 1918 Verdict

...attributed with aphrodisiac and analgesic powers, but is probably without therapeutic value.

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 408
The 2025 Reality
Status: Immune Standard

Dismissed a century ago, Echinacea is now one of the world's most researched herbs. It is widely accepted as an immunostimulant, used globally to reduce the duration and severity of colds and upper respiratory infections. (Source: Immunostimulant Clinical Review)

Witch Hazel

Hamamelis virginiana
The 1918 Verdict

...seems to possess no active physiological properties.

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 523
The 2025 Reality
Status: FDA Recognized

The 1918 editors were mistaken. Today, Witch Hazel is an FDA-recognized over-the-counter drug ingredient. Its tannins provide proven astringent and hemostatic effects, making it a standard treatment for skin irritation and hemorrhoids. (Source: FDA OTC Monograph)

Marigold

Calendula officinalis
The 1918 Verdict

...it has no virtues beyond that of a feeble aromatic.

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 268
The 2025 Reality
Status: Clinical Vulnerary

Far from "feeble," Calendula is a powerhouse for skin repair. Modern clinical use validates its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing (vulnerary) properties, particularly for burns, dermatitis, and post-operative wound care. (Source: Wound Care Efficacy Study)

Burdock

Arctium Lappa
The 1918 Verdict

There is no sufficient reason, however, to believe it has any medicinal virtue.

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 605
The 2025 Reality
Status: Detox Powerhouse

Reclaimed by modern herbalism, Burdock is now valued as a primary "alterative" (blood purifier) and prebiotic. Its high inulin content and diuretic properties make it a staple in dermatological treatments for eczema and acne. (Source: Alterative & Prebiotic Research)

Hops

Humulus Lupulus
The 1918 Verdict

Whether this [sedative] action is due to anything more than an effect through the imagination is open to question.

Ref: USD 1918, Pg. 548
The 2025 Reality
Status: Proven Sedative

It wasn't imagination. Pharmacological studies have identified specific volatile constituents (such as methylbutenol) in Hops that exert a genuine sedative effect on the central nervous system, validating its use in sleep aids. (Source: Sedative Mechanism Analysis)

Methodology: This report compares entries from the Dispensatory of the United States of America, 20th Edition (1918) against 2025 pharmacological data and modern compendia.
